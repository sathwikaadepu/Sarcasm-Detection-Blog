Introduction

Online communication, particularly on social media platforms, is characterized by a rich tapestry of expressions, including the subtle and often challenging-to-detect phenomenon of sarcasm. In response to the growing need for nuanced sentiment analysis, this project introduces an innovative multi-modal ensemble approach to sarcasm detection. By integrating both textual and audio information sourced from social media content, the framework aims to enhance the accuracy and robustness of sarcasm detection in diverse online conversations.

In conclusion, this project not only contributes to the advancement of sarcasm detection on social media platforms but also underscores the significance of a multi-modal approach. By considering both linguistic and acoustic cues, the framework offers a more holistic understanding of sarcasm, catering to the complexities of communication in the dynamic landscape of online platforms.

Literature Review

Sarcasm is like a form of wordplay where the words mean the opposite of what they say. People often use it to be funny, make a point, or tease someone. Figuring out if someone is being sarcastic can be tough for computer systems because it's not just about the words but also understanding the context and tone. Sarcasm isn't limited to just written words; it can also be spoken or a mix of both. That's why researchers are focusing on figuring out sarcasm not only in text but also in audio or a combination of both. Detecting sarcasm in different types of information sources, like text and audio, has become a hot topic in recent research on language processing.


Text-based sarcasm detection involves teaching computers to recognize sarcasm in written content like social media posts or news articles. There are three main approaches: rule-based methods rely on specific rules and patterns such as words or punctuation marks, machine learning methods use various features like words, sentence structure, and sentiment to train computers to recognize sarcasm, and deep learning methods employ advanced algorithms like neural networks to understand high-level patterns. These approaches have shown promise in various situations, but they also face challenges like a lack of labeled data, the diversity and ambiguity of sarcasm expressions, the need for contextual information, and a lack of standardized evaluation metrics. The goal is to make computers really good at catching sarcasm in diverse online conversations.


Detecting sarcasm in audio involves teaching computers to understand when someone is speaking sarcastically in various situations like podcasts, videos, or dialogues. There are two main ways to do this: one focuses on the sound itself, analyzing features like pitch and intensity of the voice, while the other looks at the words being spoken, considering aspects like the meaning and structure of the speech. These methods have potential applications in sentiment analysis, opinion mining, chatbot development, and cyberbullying detection. However, challenges include the complexity of speech signals, potential noise in audio data, subjective interpretations of sarcasm, and a lack of standardized ways to measure the performance of sarcasm detection in audio. Despite these difficulties, advancements in audio-based sarcasm detection could significantly improve our ability to comprehend and respond to sarcastic speech in various contexts.

Mustard Dataset:

They created something called the MUStARD dataset, which is like a special collection of videos for studying how computers can find sarcasm. They took clips from popular TV shows like Friends, The Golden Girls, and The Big Bang Theory, as well as a show we made up called Sarcasmaholics Anonymous. In each video clip, people say things, and they labeled whether what they said was sarcastic or not. We also added extra information about the situation around each thing they said. This dataset helps researchers teach computers to understand and detect sarcasm in videos.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the Multi-modal Ensemble-Based Sarcasm Detection Framework has successfully addressed the intricate challenge of identifying sarcasm across text and audio modalities. Leveraging neural network techniques for text analysis and a Random Forest classifier for audio signals, the project achieved commendable accuracy and demonstrated the potential for further enhancement through multimodal fusion. The results underscore the significance of integrating diverse cues for robust sarcasm detection. Future directions include exploring advanced fusion techniques, real-time deployment, user-friendly interfaces, and continuous learning mechanisms, positioning this framework as a valuable tool for nuanced sentiment analysis in the dynamic landscape of social media communication.

Future Scope

The successful implementation of the sarcasm detection framework lays the groundwork for several exciting avenues of exploration and enhancement:

Multimodal Fusion Strategies:

Explore advanced multimodal fusion techniques to further integrate information from text and audio modalities. Investigate methods such as attention mechanisms or transformer-based architectures to capture nuanced relationships between textual and auditory sarcasm cues.

Model Fine-tuning:

Conduct extensive hyperparameter tuning and fine-tuning of models to optimize performance on diverse datasets and social media contexts. This involves exploring different neural network architectures, adjusting learning rates, and experimenting with various preprocessing techniques for both text and audio data.

Real-time Detection:

Investigate the feasibility of implementing real-time sarcasm detection on social media platforms. This involves optimizing models for low-latency predictions and exploring efficient deployment strategies, considering the dynamic nature of content generation on social media.

Adversarial Testing:

Assess the robustness of the model against adversarial attacks. Investigate techniques to enhance the model's resilience in real-world scenarios where users may intentionally craft content to deceive sarcasm detection algorithms.

Expand Dataset Diversity:

Extend the dataset to encompass a more diverse range of social media sources, linguistic styles, and cultural contexts. This ensures the model's generalization across various demographics and language variations, making it more applicable in a global context.

Incremental Model Updates:

Implement a mechanism for continuous learning, allowing the model to adapt to evolving language trends and variations in sarcasm expression over time. Regularly update the model with new labeled data to enhance its understanding of emerging sarcasm patterns.
